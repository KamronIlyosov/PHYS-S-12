Final Project: Electricity-Generating Leg Brace

Check-In & Progress Log

Overview: I’m designing a leg brace that generates electricity as you walk, converting mechanical energy from leg motion into usable electrical power. The end goal is to create a wearable device that can power small electronics such as phones.

Progress So Far:

  • Motor Update: Replaced the original DC motor with a stepper motor to reduce gear jamming and mechanical noise. Stepper motors are also better suited for stable energy harvesting.
  • Pulley & Belt Transmission: Developed a pulley system to transfer motion. Initial pulley sizes didn’t generate enough torque, so I designed and 3D printed larger and smaller pulleys to increase the gear ratio.
  • Electrical Circuit: Built a dual-bridge rectifier system to convert AC output from both sets of stepper motor coils into DC. Added capacitors for voltage smoothing, a 5V linear voltage regulator, and LEDs to test voltage output.
  • Voltage Regulation: Linear regulators proved inefficient with varying input voltage. While I found some buck converters in the lab, I realized that a buck-boost converter is ideal since it can handle both voltage drops and spikes to maintain a steady 5V output.
  • Mechanical Fit: Designed a curved mount in CAD to match the shape of a calf. This component will help securely attach the brace while maintaining comfort during walking.

Goals for Wednesday & Thursday:

  • Have the full mechanical + electrical system working together while walking.
  • Achieve a consistent and usable 5V output with enough current to charge a device.
  • Begin testing with a buck-boost converter under real walking conditions.
